The famous speech of Swami Vivekananda that is widely known and celebrated is his address at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago on September 11, 1893. In this iconic speech, Swami Vivekananda began with the words "Sisters and Brothers of America," which resonated with the audience and set the tone for his message of universal brotherhood, religious tolerance, and the acceptance of all faiths.
Here is an excerpt from his speech:
"It fills my heart with joy unspeakable to rise in response to the warm and cordial welcome which you have given us. I thank you in the name of the most ancient order of monks in the world; I thank you in the name of the mother of religions, and I thank you in the name of millions and millions of Hindu people of all classes and sects."
Swami Vivekananda's speech emphasized the importance of tolerance, acceptance, and the recognition of the underlying unity of all humanity. He spoke about the need to embrace diversity, practice tolerance, and respect the beliefs of others. His message of universalism and the spiritual essence of Hinduism left a lasting impression and continues to inspire people around the world to this day.
